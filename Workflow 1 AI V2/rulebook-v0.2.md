**B — Situation.** B1 **Onset/Duration** [FIXED]: acute · chronic · recurrent · post-event. · B2 **Severity** [FIXED]: mild · moderate · severe · persistent · emergency-flag. · B3 **Demographic/Context** [OPEN]. · B4 **Trigger/Context** [OPEN]. · B5 **Awareness Level** [FIXED]: unaware · problem-aware · solution-aware · product-aware · most-aware (placement *input*, not the placement).
**C — Action/Relationship.** C1 **Primary Action** [FIXED]: define/learn · identify-symptom · find-cause · assess-risk · get-diagnosis · learn-prognosis · find-treatment · compare-options · evaluate-specific-product · find-where-to-buy · learn-usage/dosage · check-safety/side-effects · seek-reassurance · find-support · manage/cope. · C2 **Concern/Driver** [FIXED]: is-it-serious · is-it-normal · will-it-last · how-long-to-heal · will-it-recur · is-it-curable · will-it-work · is-it-safe · how-much-cost · how-fast · why-me. · C3 **Commercial Orientation** [FIXED]: informational · commercial-investigation · transactional.

## 2. Zone model (coarse funnel stages — the macro vertical order)
1 **Awareness** → 2 **Problem Exploration** → 3 **Cause & Diagnosis** → 4 **Solution Research** → 5 **Evaluation** → 6 **Decision & Purchase** → 7 **Post-Purchase**. (This sequence *is* the macro vertical order; see §10.)

## 3. Stage order within each zone (the meso vertical order — listed in canonical journey order = stage rank; locked, editable)
1. **Awareness** → general-noticing.
2. **Problem Exploration** → symptom-identification → seriousness → normalization → by-location/variants → recovery-expectation.
3. **Cause & Diagnosis** → cause-understanding → risk/causation → differential → test/validation.
4. **Solution Research** → treatment-discovery → mechanism → modality-comparison (natural vs medical).
5. **Evaluation** → option-vs-option → specific-product-assessment → efficacy/reviews.
6. **Decision & Purchase** → brand-choice → price → where-to-buy.
7. **Post-Purchase** → usage/dosage → side-effects/safety → recovery-timeline → recurrence-prevention.

## 4. Value ladders (template — authored per niche)
General→specific, so shells can be generated by climbing them. Body Location: site → region → limb/system → whole-body (*"prepatellar bursa" → "knee" → "lower limb" → systemic*). Named Entity where applicable: item → class → modality (*"ibuprofen" → "NSAID" → pharmacological*).

## 5. Placement rules (intent profile → zone/stage — generic; first match wins; unmatched → needs-placement queue)
- **R1** symptom + (identify/define) → Problem Exploration (symptom-identification).
- **R2** find-cause OR Subject=cause → Cause & Diagnosis (cause-understanding).
- **R3** get-diagnosis OR Subject=diagnosis, OR a *condition-vs-related-condition* "which/or" framing → Cause & Diagnosis (differential/validation) (*"[condition] vs [related condition]"*).
- **R4** Subject=risk-factor OR (assess-risk + Concern∈{is-it-serious, will-it-recur}) → Cause & Diagnosis (risk).
- **R5** seek-reassurance OR (symptom + Concern∈{is-it-normal, is-it-serious}) → Problem Exploration (symptom-normalization).
- **R6** find-treatment, no comparison cue → Solution Research (treatment-discovery).
- **R7** compare-options OR Subject=comparison OR Awareness=product-aware OR "best/top/vs" cue → Evaluation (option-vs-option).
- **R8** evaluate-specific-product → Evaluation (specific-product).
- **R9** find-where-to-buy OR Commercial=transactional OR Subject=cost → Decision & Purchase.
- **R10** learn-usage/dosage OR check-safety/side-effects → Post-Purchase (regardless of entity) (*"[treatment] side effects" → Post-Purchase*).
- **R11** Concern∈{how-long-to-heal, will-it-recur}, no purchase/usage cue → **primary: Problem Exploration (recovery-expectation); secondary affinity: Post-Purchase (recovery/recurrence)** — *resolves Q-RB-A:* the worry first surfaces early, where answering it builds trust and pulls the searcher down the funnel; the topic is cross-referenced to Post-Purchase so content can also serve it after they act.

**Precedence:** explicit purchase/usage/side-effect cues (R9, R10) > comparison cue "best/vs" (R7) > generic treatment (R6); otherwise **Action (C1) > Subject Type (A1)**.

**Misfit taxonomy (what happens when a topic doesn't fit a zone — never guess; route to the needs-placement queue, then resolve by type):**
1. *No rule matched, intent clear (rule gap)* → add a placement rule.
2. *Genuinely straddles two zones* → one **primary zone** (by precedence) + a **secondary "also-relevant-in" affinity** (as R11).
3. *Zone clear, no stage fits* → place under a generic stage in the zone; flag a possible new stage.
4. *No zone fits at all* → an **adjacent/holding area**; a cluster signals a possible new zone (*e.g., "Living With / Support"*) or that the intent is out-of-scope for this niche.
5. *Too ambiguous to place* → route to re-review, don't force.

## 6. Ignorable set + normalization (carrier dedup)
Ignorable = the condition term (per project) + closed stopwords **{for, the, a}** only — never prepositions (in/on/from/with/after) or qualifiers (all/best/top/natural/home). Normalize: lemmatize plurals; normalize word order for the dedup key; map niche **aliases** to the canonical condition term (*"housemaid's knee" → prepatellar bursitis*; alias list in the niche layer).

## 7. Naming & boundary conventions
**Title in the searcher's voice** — the headline of the content they want (*"Remedies for left ankle bursitis," "Is bursitis serious?"*); **neutral** title only when first-person is unnatural (clinical/third-party/comparison: *"How doctors diagnose [condition]"*). Tag each `searcher`/`neutral`. **Boundary:** contrastive "belongs / does NOT belong (→ nearest neighbor)," naming a concrete neighbor.

## 8. Merge & nest policy (conservative)
Merge **only on identical profiles** (after normalization). **Containment is nesting, not merging** — an extra explicit aspect makes an intent distinct; the more specific nests under the broader (*"…in left knee" vs "…pain in left knee" → distinct, nested*). Bias to over-split. **Shells:** generate by climbing ladders (§4); **demand-aware** (only if a keyword maps to it or it's a natural grouping level); empty shells allowed.

## 9. Diagnostic & convergence (how the niche layer is produced) — CONFIRMED
- **Sample:** ~500 as a **blend** — top 300 by volume + 200 for diversity across phrasing/length — a **floor**; convergence decides whether to pull more for vocabulary-rich niches.
- **Convergence to proceed:** out-of-vocab < 5% · coverage-gap < 2% · placement-miss < 5% · inter-pass agreement > 85%.
- **Emits:** out-of-vocab flags · coverage gaps · placement misses · ambiguity hotspots.
- **Scope & promotion:** niche-scoped by default; promote to Universal once recurring in ≥ 2 niches.
- **In scope for the first build:** the rulebook is **DB-backed data** and the **automated diagnostic** runs in core; only the human-correction feedback loop (Lessons Module UI) is the fast-follow.

## 10. Ordering (vertical & horizontal — finalized)
**Principle.** Vertical order (down the funnel) is **journey-driven, never volume-driven** — a popular "buy" topic does not rise just because it's popular. Horizontal order (siblings at the same level) is **priority-driven (search volume)**, *except* where siblings have a **natural sequence**, which wins.
**Computation (deterministic sort — no per-topic AI; works at any depth).**
- `verticalRank` = (**zone rank** §2, then **stage rank** §3). 
- `siblingOrder` = a **natural-sequence hint** if one applies to the group, else **descending volume**.
- AI ordering is a fallback only for a sibling group flagged ambiguous.
**Natural-sequence hints (sequence beats volume):** treatment options → conservative→aggressive (*rest/ice → therapy → medication → injection → surgery*); severity → mild→severe; a laddered axis (e.g., location) → by the ladder or by volume (niche choice). Elsewhere → volume.
**Spine:** the top-level major parents, ordered by §2; everything else hangs beneath, ordered by the same two rules at each level.

## 11. Reorganization sweeps (periodic, independent self-correction during assembly)
As topics are placed, run **independent reorganization sweeps at a regular cadence** (default: every batch-wave / every ~N topics, plus a final pass; cadence is a config knob) that re-evaluate the structure and fix drift:
- split oversized topics; merge/relocate tiny or orphaned ones; re-nest a topic under a better parent as more-specific topics arrive; re-rank siblings as relative volumes shift; prune dead shells / create missing ones; re-verify zone/stage placement (§5) and spine composition (§10).
- **Critical constraint:** a sweep operates on the **condensed skeleton** (titles + zone/stage tags + volumes + parent links) and on **small local slices** (one parent's children at a time) — it **must not re-feed the entire raw tree into a single prompt**. This keeps it cheap and preserves the flat-payload principle (unlike Variant A's whole-canvas consolidation pass).
- most checks are mechanical or per-slice and parallel; ambiguous calls → needs-review queue.
